Description

Curry is a dish with a sauce or gravy seasoned with spices, mainly derived from the interchange of Indian cuisine with European taste in food, starting with the Portuguese, followed by the Dutch and British, and then thoroughly internationalised. Many dishes that would be described as curries in English are found in the native cuisines of countries in Southeast Asia and East Asia.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp. olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp. curry powder
  • 1 tsp. cinnamon
  • 1 tsp. paprika
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 tsp. ginger root, grated
  • 1/2 tsp. white sugar
  • salt to taste
  • 2 skinless boneless chicken breast halves, cut bite-sized
  • 1 tbsp. tomato paste
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 3/4 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 lemon, juiced
  • 1/2 tsp. cayenne

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan on low heat, simmer onion until browned.
  2. Stir in garlic, curry powder, cinnamon, paprika, bay leaf, ginger, sugar, salt. Stir for 2 minutes.
  3. Add chicken, tomato, yogurt, and coconut milk. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
  4. Remove bay leaf, add lemon juice and cayenne. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. Serve hot with equal portions white rice.
