Description

A pancake, also known as a hotcake, griddlecake, or flapjack, is a flat type of batter bread like cake, often thin and round, prepared from a starch-based batter that may contain eggs, milk, and butter, and then cooked on a hot surface such as a griddle or frying pan. Archaeological evidence suggests that pancakes were probably eaten in prehistoric societies.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 1/2 tsp. baking powder
  • 1 tbsp. white sugar
  • 1/4 tsp. salt, or more to taste
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 3 tbsp. butter, melted
  • 1 large egg

Steps

  1. Stir flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt together in a large bowl.
  2. Add milk, butter, and egg, stirring in slowly until smooth.
  3. Heat a griddle or pan over medium-high heat.
  4. Pour ~1/4 cup batter per pancake, cooking until edges are brown.
  5. Flip pancakes, then remove when fully cooked.
  6. Serve hot, with butter, maple syrup, or fruit.
